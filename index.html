Before you go — Maya & Salo
  • Book the big ones now. Lima fine-dining and Cusco's top plaza spots fill fast. The Splurge tier (Central, Maido, Kjolle) books 6–10 weeks out — for a Jun 30 arrival, try immediately or treat as a maybe.
  • Your dates dodge most closures. Lima falls on Wed–Sat, so the Sun/Mon-closed spots (Central, Maido, Kjolle, Awicha, Nuestro) are all open while you're there. In Cusco, Parada Vegana is closed Sun (Jul 5). Closed-today badges update live on each card.
  • Altitude in Cusco (~3,400m). Eat light your first day and go easy on alcohol; most spots serve coca or muña tea for soroche.
  • Maya — no red meat. Trout (trucha), chicken, and all the seafood/ceviche spots are safe, plus the 🌿 veg-forward picks (filter "Healthy"). Cuy and alpaca are red meat — flagged ⚠ in notes.
  • Paracas is a small fishing town. Stick to the higher-rated rooms below for seafood freshness; a couple of waterfront spots have a shaky reputation.
  • Everything is tappable → opens in Google Maps, with Directions and Call on each card.
⚠ Allergen note — please read
  • No allergy profile on file for Maya or Salo. If either has a nut or soy allergy, the Nikkei and Chifa picks are soy-heavy, and some criollo dishes use peanut (e.g. Pukasoncco's peanut chicken in Paracas). Confirm preparation with each restaurant before ordering.
